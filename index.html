Here's a timeline of the life and contributions of Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi:

  • 780 - Muhammad ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi is born in Khwarezm (now Khiva, Uzbekistan), a region in Central Asia.
  • 800s - Al-Khwarizmi likely received his education in Baghdad, which was a center of learning during the Abbasid Caliphate.
  • 820 - Al-Khwarizmi writes his most famous work, "Kitab al-Jabr wa-l-Muqabala" (The Compendious Book on Calculation by Completion and Balancing), which introduces systematic solutions of linear and quadratic equations. This work laid the foundations of algebra and gave the subject its name.
  • 830 - Al-Khwarizmi writes "Al-Kitab al-Mukhtasar fi Hisab al-Jabr wal-Muqabala" (The Compendious Book on Calculation by Completion and Balancing), which is an introductory book on algebra and arithmetic. This work introduced the Hindu-Arabic numeral system to the Islamic world, which later spread to Europe and became the basis of modern arithmetic.
  • 830-850 - Al-Khwarizmi serves as the head of the House of Wisdom (Bayt al-Hikma) in Baghdad, a major intellectual center during the Islamic Golden Age.
  • 830-860 - Al-Khwarizmi works on astronomy and geography, producing tables of trigonometric functions and updating Ptolemaic astronomical theories.
  • 850 - Al-Khwarizmi writes "Kitab surat al-ard" (The Image of the Earth), a work on geography and cartography, which provided the coordinates of many cities and discussed methods for drawing maps.
  • 850-870 - Al-Khwarizmi's works are translated into Latin and become influential in medieval Europe, contributing to the European Renaissance and the advancement of mathematics.
  • 860 - Al-Khwarizmi likely dies in Baghdad or possibly in Khwarezm.

    • Al-Khwarizmi's legacy endures through his works, which not only preserved the knowledge of ancient civilizations but also expanded upon it, laying the groundwork for the development of modern mathematics and science.
